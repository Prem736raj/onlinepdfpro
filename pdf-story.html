The Birth of PDF

From a vision of a paperless world to the most used document format on the planet.

Scroll to begin
1991

The Camelot Project

It started with a memo. Dr. John Warnock, co-founder of Adobe, launched a project called "Camelot". His goal was to solve a fundamental problem: how to share documents between different computers and operating systems without losing formatting.

The Vision: "Imagine being able to send full text and graphics documents over a network... and have them viewable on any machine."
1993

The World Meets PDF

At the COMDEX conference in 1993, Adobe officially released PDF 1.0 (Portable Document Format). At the time, it was a proprietary format, and Adobe Acrobat (the software to read it) cost $50 per user!

In those days, the web was just beginning, and sharing 2MB files was a massive challenge over slow dial-up modems.

2008

Set Free

For 15 years, Adobe controlled the PDF specification. But in 2008, everything changed. Adobe released PDF as an open standard (ISO 32000-1).

This meant any developer in the world could create software to read, write, and edit PDFs without paying Adobe. This was the moment PDF truly became the language of the internet.

Today

The Universal Language

Today, billions of PDF files are created every year. From tax forms and legal contracts to interactive e-books and high-end printing, PDF is the bridge that connects every device on Earth.
