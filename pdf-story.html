The story of how information became universal.

1991

The Camelot Vision

Before PDF, sharing a document was a nightmare. If the other person didn't have your exact fonts or software, your work looked broken.

John Warnock saw a future where documents were independent. He wrote a memo titled "The Camelot Project," envisioning a format that would look perfect on any screen, anywhere.

1993

The Launch

PDF 1.0 was born. But it wasn't an instant hit. The software to read it cost $50, and files were massive for the slow internet of the 90s.

2008

Freedom for PDF

This is the most important date in PDF history. Adobe handed over the keys, making PDF an Open Standard (ISO 32000-1).

Suddenly, anyone could build PDF tools without permission. This explosion of creativity is what allows sites like OnlinePDFPro to exist today.
