Why Convert PowerPoint to PDF?

  • Consistent rendering: PDFs display identically everywhere, unlike PowerPoint which may shift layouts when fonts are missing
  • Smaller file size: PPT files with embedded media can be 50-200MB. PDFs are typically much smaller
  • No software required: Recipients can view PDFs in any browser without needing PowerPoint
  • Professional sharing: PDFs prevent accidental edits and look more polished for client deliverables
  • Print-ready: PDFs produce reliable print output, essential for handouts and materials

Best Practices for PPT to PDF Conversion

Use Standard Fonts

Stick to widely available fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman. Custom fonts may not render correctly in browser-based conversion.

Check Slide Dimensions

Standard 16:9 slides convert well. If you use custom dimensions, verify the PDF layout matches your expectations.

Remove Speaker Notes if Not Needed

Speaker notes add extra pages to the PDF. If you only need the visual slides, remove notes before converting.

Compress After Conversion

If your PDF is too large (especially with image-heavy slides), run it through our Compress PDF tool.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert .pptx and .ppt files?▼

Yes. Both the modern .pptx format and legacy .ppt format are supported.

Will animations be preserved?▼

No — PDF is a static format. Animations and transitions are not included. Each slide becomes a static page.

Is the conversion private?▼

Yes. Your presentation is processed in your browser. No data is sent to any server.

Can I convert the PDF back to PowerPoint?▼

Yes! Use our PDF to PPT tool to convert PDF pages back into editable PowerPoint slides.

What about embedded videos?▼

Videos cannot be embedded in PDFs. The video frame will appear as a static image.
