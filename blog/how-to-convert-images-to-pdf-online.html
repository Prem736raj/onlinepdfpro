Why Convert Images to PDF?

  • Document compilation: Combine multiple scanned pages, receipts, or certificates into a single organized file
  • Universal sharing: PDFs open on every device without requiring image viewers or specific apps
  • Professional presentation: PDFs provide a polished format for reports, portfolios, and proposals that contain visual content
  • Size control: You can compress the resulting PDF to meet email or portal size limits
  • Page ordering: Arrange images in the exact order you want — something that's hard to guarantee when sharing individual image files

Supported Image Formats

  • JPEG/JPG: The most common photo format. Ideal for photographs and complex images with gradients
  • PNG: Best for screenshots, diagrams, and images with text or sharp edges. Supports transparency
  • WEBP: Modern format with excellent compression. Common in web downloads
  • BMP: Uncompressed bitmap format. Large files but zero quality loss

Tips for Best Quality PDFs

Compress Images Before Converting

If your images are very large (5MB+ each), the resulting PDF will be massive. Use our Image Compress tool to optimize images before converting to PDF.

Use Consistent Orientation

For a professional-looking PDF, ensure all images use the same orientation. Mix of portrait and landscape images can look awkward when scrolling through the document.

Scan at Appropriate Resolution

For documents meant to be read on screen, 150 DPI is sufficient. For print-quality output, use 300 DPI. Higher resolutions produce larger files without visible quality improvement on most screens.
