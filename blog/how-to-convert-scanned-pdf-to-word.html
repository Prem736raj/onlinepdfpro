What is a Scanned PDF?

A scanned PDF is created when you scan a physical document using a scanner or phone camera. Unlike a digitally-created PDF (from Word or Google Docs), a scanned PDF stores each page as an image. This means the "text" you see is actually pixels in a photograph — your computer can't distinguish letters from the background.

Signs that your PDF is scanned:

  • You can't select or highlight text with your mouse
  • Ctrl+F (Find) doesn't work
  • The text looks slightly fuzzy or has a grainy background
  • The file size is unusually large (pages are stored as full images)

How OCR Technology Works

OCR (Optical Character Recognition) analyzes images to identify text characters. Modern OCR engines like Tesseract (which powers our tool) use machine learning to:

  1. Detect text regions: Identify areas of the image that contain text vs. images or whitespace
  2. Segment characters: Break text regions into individual characters
  3. Recognize patterns: Match each character shape against a trained database of fonts and handwriting
  4. Apply language models: Use dictionary lookups and context to improve accuracy (e.g., correcting "th3" to "the")

🔑 Accuracy Note

Modern OCR achieves 95-99% accuracy on clean, printed text. Handwritten text, unusual fonts, or poor-quality scans will have lower accuracy.

Tips for Better OCR Accuracy

Scan at 300 DPI or Higher

Higher resolution scans give OCR engines more detail to work with. 300 DPI is the sweet spot for text documents — providing clear character definition without excessive file sizes.

Ensure Good Contrast

Black text on white background produces the best results. Low-contrast documents (gray text, colored backgrounds) reduce accuracy significantly.

Straighten the Document

Skewed or rotated scans confuse OCR engines. Use our Rotate PDF tool to straighten pages before OCR processing.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can OCR extract text from handwriting?▼

OCR works best with printed text. Handwriting recognition has lower accuracy but can work for neat, clear handwriting. Results vary significantly based on handwriting legibility.

Is my scanned document uploaded to a server?▼

No. Our OCR tool processes your document entirely in your browser using the Tesseract.js library. Your sensitive documents stay on your device.

How accurate is the text extraction?▼

For clean, well-scanned printed documents at 300 DPI, accuracy is typically 95-99%. Lower quality scans or unusual fonts may reduce accuracy.

Can I OCR a multi-page scanned PDF?▼

Yes. Each page is processed sequentially. The extracted text from all pages is combined in the output.

Why is some text missing or wrong?▼

Common causes include low scan quality, unusual fonts, watermarks over text, or selecting the wrong language. Try re-scanning at higher quality.
