Why No App Is Needed

OnlinePDFPro is a Progressive Web App (PWA) that runs entirely in your browser. It uses your iPhone's built-in processing power to combine PDFs — no download, no account, no subscription. You can even install it as a home screen shortcut for instant access, but it's completely optional.

🔒 Privacy on iPhone

Your PDFs are processed locally on your device using Safari's JavaScript engine. No files are uploaded to any server — what happens on your iPhone stays on your iPhone.

Step-by-Step: Merge PDF on iPhone

1

Open Safari

Open Safari on your iPhone and navigate to onlinepdfpro.com/tools/merge-pdf.html.

2

Tap "Choose Files"

Tap the upload button. iOS will show you options: "Photo Library," "Take Photo," or "Browse." Tap "Browse" to open the Files app.

3

Select Your PDFs

Navigate to the PDFs in your Files app. To select multiple files, tap "Select" in the top-right corner, then tap each PDF you want to merge. Tap "Open" when done.

4

Arrange and Merge

Drag files to reorder them (tap and hold to drag on mobile). When the order is correct, tap the "Merge" button.

5

Download

Tap "Download" and the merged PDF will save to your Downloads folder in the Files app. You can then share it via email, AirDrop, WhatsApp, or any other app.

Tips for iPhone Users

Save to Files for Easy Access

When you receive PDFs via email or Messages, tap and hold the attachment, then choose "Save to Files." This makes them easier to find when using the merge tool.

Install as Home Screen App

In Safari, tap the share button (square with arrow) → "Add to Home Screen." This creates an app icon for instant access to all OnlinePDFPro tools.

Works Offline Too

Once the page loads, the merge tool works without internet — perfect for situations with poor connectivity.

Alternative Methods on iOS

Apple Files App (iOS 15+)

The Files app has a built-in "Create PDF" feature. Select multiple images → tap the three-dot menu → "Create PDF." However, this only works with images, not existing PDFs.

Apple Shortcuts App

You can create a custom Shortcut to merge PDFs, but it requires technical setup and doesn't allow reordering pages easily.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Does it work on iPad too?▼

Yes! The tool works identically on iPad in both Safari and Chrome. The larger screen makes drag-to-reorder even easier.

Can I merge more than 2 PDFs?▼

Yes. You can add as many PDFs as you need. There is no limit.

Why should I use this instead of an app?▼

No storage space used, no account required, no subscription fees, no ads in the tool, and your files stay private (never uploaded to servers).

Does it work on Chrome for iOS?▼

Yes. The tool works on any iOS browser including Safari, Chrome, Firefox, and Edge.

What if the download doesn't work?▼

On some iOS versions, Safari may block downloads. Go to Settings → Safari → Downloads → select "On My iPhone" as the download location. Then retry.
