Why Do You Need to Convert Aadhaar PDF to JPG?

Several real-world situations require your Aadhaar in image format instead of PDF:

  • Government job applications: SSC, UPSC, Railways, and state PSC portals require identity documents as JPG/JPEG images with specific size limits (often 50KB-200KB)
  • Competitive exam registrations: GATE, CAT, JEE, NEET, and CLAT application forms ask for scanned documents in image format
  • University admissions: DU, JNU, IIT, and private university portals often accept only image uploads for ID verification
  • Bank account opening: Some online banking KYC processes require Aadhaar as a JPEG image
  • Scholarship applications: National Scholarship Portal and state scholarship websites need ID documents in image format
  • SIM card activation: Telecom operators sometimes ask for Aadhaar in image format for digital KYC

File Size Requirements for Common Indian Portals

Here are the most common file size requirements you'll encounter when uploading Aadhaar images:

  • SSC (Staff Selection Commission): ID proof as JPG, 50KB-100KB
  • UPSC: Identity document as JPEG, max 300KB
  • IBPS (Banking exams): Photo ID as JPG, 20KB-50KB
  • GATE: Category certificate and ID as JPEG, max 200KB
  • NEET: ID proof as JPG, 50KB-300KB
  • National Scholarship Portal: Aadhaar image, max 200KB
  • Passport Seva: Supporting documents as JPEG, max 1MB

Tips for Best Quality Results

Download Fresh Copy from UIDAI

If your existing Aadhaar PDF is a photo of a printout (taken with a phone camera), the quality will be poor. Instead, download a fresh digital copy from myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in or the mAadhaar app. Digital copies are much cleaner and convert to sharper JPG images.

Use High DPI Settings

When converting PDF to images, higher DPI (dots per inch) produces sharper images but larger files. For exam forms that only need readability, 150 DPI is sufficient. For official submissions where you need the text to be crystal clear, use 300 DPI.

Crop Unnecessary Margins

Use our Image Crop tool to remove white margins around the Aadhaar card. This reduces file size and focuses the image on the actual content — portals often reject uploads with excessive whitespace.
