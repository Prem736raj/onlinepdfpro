Understanding PDF Password Types

PDFs can have two types of passwords:

  • User password (open password): Required to open and view the PDF. Without this password, you cannot see any content. Our tool requires you to enter this password to process the file.
  • Owner password (permissions password): Restricts actions like printing, copying text, or editing. The PDF can be viewed without this password, but certain features are locked. Our tool can remove these restrictions.

⚠️ Important

Our tool is designed for legitimate use — removing passwords from documents you own or have authorization to unlock. We do not support breaking encryption on documents you don't have the password for.

Common Scenarios for Unlocking PDFs

Downloaded Bank Statements

Banks often password-protect PDF statements with your date of birth or account number. You know the password but want an unlocked version for your records or to share with your accountant.

Aadhaar Card PDFs

Aadhaar PDFs downloaded from UIDAI are password-protected. The password is typically your PIN code or first 4 letters of name + birth year. Unlock it before converting to JPG.

Old Documents

You set a password on a document years ago and now need unrestricted access. Enter the password once and save an unprotected copy.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I remove a password I don't know?▼

No. For PDFs with an open password, you must enter the correct password. Our tool removes the protection after authentication, it does not crack passwords.

Is this legal?▼

Yes, when done on documents you own or are authorized to access. Removing passwords from documents you don't have permission to access may violate laws.

Will the content be changed?▼

No. The tool only removes the password protection layer. All content, formatting, and metadata remain identical.

Does it work with all encryption types?▼

Our tool supports standard PDF encryption (40-bit RC4, 128-bit RC4, 128-bit AES, and 256-bit AES). Very old or non-standard encryption may not be supported.

Can I re-add password protection later?▼

Yes. Use our PDF Protect tool to add new password protection to any PDF.
