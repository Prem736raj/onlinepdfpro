Page numbers seem like a small detail, but they make a big difference in professional documents. Without them, a 30-page report becomes impossible to reference in meetings ("turn to page... uh, somewhere in the middle"). Legal documents, academic papers, and business reports all require proper page numbering.

Why Add Page Numbers?

  • Professional appearance: Numbered pages signal a polished, complete document
  • Easy reference: "See page 14" is much more helpful than "somewhere near the middle"
  • Print navigation: Essential when printing multi-page documents for physical distribution
  • Meeting materials: Everyone can follow along when the presenter says "turn to page 7"
  • Academic requirements: Most academic style guides (APA, MLA, Chicago) require page numbers

Customization Options

Position

Choose from 6 positions: top-left, top-center, top-right, bottom-left, bottom-center (most common), or bottom-right.

Number Format

Options include Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3), Roman numerals (i, ii, iii), or "Page X of Y" format for documents where total count matters.

Starting Number

Start from any number — useful when your PDF is part of a larger document. If your section starts at page 15, set the starting number accordingly.

Skip First Page

Many documents (reports, papers) have a cover page that shouldn't be numbered. Enable "Skip first page" to start numbering from page 2.

Tips for Professional Documents

Add Numbers After Merging

If you're combining multiple PDFs with Merge PDF, add page numbers after merging for continuous sequential numbering.

Use "Page X of Y" for Legal Documents

The "Page 1 of 15" format is preferred for contracts and legal documents as it confirms the total page count, preventing page removal or addition.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I skip numbering on certain pages?▼

You can skip the first page (cover page). For more selective skipping, split the PDF, number each section separately, then merge.

Will page numbers overlap existing content?▼

Page numbers are placed in the margin area. If your PDF has content extending into the margins, consider adjusting the position.

Can I change the font of page numbers?▼

The tool uses a clean, standard font that works well with all documents. Custom font selection may be available in future updates.

Does it work with scanned PDFs?▼

Yes. Page numbers are added as a new layer on top of each page, regardless of whether the PDF is scanned or digital.

Can I remove page numbers later?▼

Once added, page numbers are embedded in the PDF. Keep your original unnumbered version as a backup.
