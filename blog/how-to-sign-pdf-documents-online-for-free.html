Legal Validity of E-Signatures

Electronic signatures are legally valid in most countries:

  • United States: ESIGN Act (2000) gives e-signatures the same legal standing as handwritten signatures
  • European Union: eIDAS Regulation recognizes electronic signatures across all EU member states
  • India: Information Technology Act (2000) recognizes electronic signatures and digital signatures
  • United Kingdom: Electronic Communications Act (2000) validates e-signatures
  • Canada, Australia, Japan: All have legislation recognizing electronic signatures

✅ Note

Simple electronic signatures (like those created with our tool) are valid for most business and personal documents. Some high-security documents (real estate, court filings) may require qualified digital signatures with certificates.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is a drawn signature legally binding?▼

Yes, in most jurisdictions. The ESIGN Act (US), eIDAS (EU), and IT Act (India) all recognize electronic signatures as legally equivalent to handwritten signatures for most documents.

Can I sign on my phone?▼

Yes. Use your finger to draw directly on the touchscreen. Works on both Android and iOS browsers.

Is my signed document uploaded?▼

No. Everything is processed in your browser. Your document and signature stay private on your device.

Can others edit my signature after I sign?▼

The signature is embedded in the PDF. For extra security, flatten the PDF after signing to make it non-editable.

Do I need to create an account?▼

No. No signup, no login, no subscription. Just open the tool and sign.
