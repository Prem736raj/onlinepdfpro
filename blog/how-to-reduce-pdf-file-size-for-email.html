"Your attachment is too large to send." If you've seen this frustrating error message, you're not alone. Large PDF attachments are the #1 cause of failed email sends. Every email provider has file size limits, and even a simple report with a few images can exceed them.

This guide explains exactly how to reduce your PDF to fit within any email limit — whether it's Gmail's 25MB cap, Outlook's 20MB limit, or your company's 5MB policy.

Email Attachment Size Limits

  • Gmail: 25MB per email (total for all attachments combined)
  • Outlook.com / Hotmail: 20MB per email
  • Yahoo Mail: 25MB per email
  • Apple Mail (iCloud): 20MB (uses Mail Drop for larger files)
  • Corporate Exchange servers: Often 5-10MB (set by IT administrators)
  • ProtonMail: 25MB per email

💡 Important

The limits above apply to the total size of all attachments combined, not per file. If you're attaching multiple documents, each one needs to be small enough that the total stays within the limit.

Why Are Your PDFs So Large?

  • Embedded images: High-resolution photos can add 1-5MB each to a PDF
  • Scanned documents: Each scanned page is a full-page image (200-500KB per page)
  • PowerPoint conversions: Slides with backgrounds and images create large PDFs
  • Multiple pages: A 50-page report naturally accumulates size
  • Embedded fonts: Custom fonts add 100-500KB each

Quick Fix Strategies

Strategy 1: Compress First

The simplest fix — run your PDF through our compressor. This alone typically saves 40-80% without visible quality loss.

Strategy 2: Remove Unnecessary Pages

Use Delete Pages to remove blank pages, appendices, or sections the recipient doesn't need.

Strategy 3: Split into Parts

If compression isn't enough, split the PDF into multiple smaller parts and send them in separate emails.

Strategy 4: Compress Images Before Creating PDF

If you're creating the PDF from scratch, compress your images before inserting them. This is especially effective for photo-heavy documents.

Alternative Sharing Methods for Very Large PDFs

If your PDF is truly massive (50MB+), email may not be the right channel. Consider:

  • Google Drive link: Upload to Drive and share a link instead of an attachment
  • Dropbox / OneDrive: Similar cloud sharing with shared links
  • WeTransfer: Free file transfer service for files up to 2GB
  • Direct download link: Host the file on your website and share the URL

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much can I reduce my PDF size?▼

Typical reductions range from 40-90%. Image-heavy PDFs see the most dramatic reductions. Already-optimized text PDFs may see modest 10-20% savings.

Will the quality be noticeably worse?▼

For "Balanced" compression, the difference is imperceptible at normal viewing. "Minimal Size" may show slight image quality reduction at extreme zoom levels.

Can I compress multiple PDFs for one email?▼

Yes. Upload up to 20 PDFs and compress them all at once. Download individually or as a ZIP file.

What if compression isn't enough?▼

Try removing unnecessary pages, splitting into parts, or using a cloud sharing link instead of email attachments.

Is my email content visible during compression?▼

No. Compression happens entirely in your browser. Your PDF content is never visible to us or uploaded anywhere.
