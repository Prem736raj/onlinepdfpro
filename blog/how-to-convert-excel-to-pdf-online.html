Why Convert Excel Spreadsheets to PDF?

There are several compelling reasons to convert your Excel files to PDF before sharing:

  • Universal viewing: Not everyone has Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets. PDFs open in any browser on any device without requiring special software
  • Layout preservation: PDFs render identically on every device, unlike Excel files where column widths and fonts may shift depending on the viewer's software version and screen size
  • Prevent editing: When you share a financial report, invoice, or data analysis, you often don't want recipients to modify the numbers. PDF format makes the document read-only
  • Professional appearance: PDFs look more polished for client presentations, formal reports, and official submissions
  • Smaller file size: Excel files with heavy formatting can be surprisingly large. PDFs are typically more compact, especially after compression
  • Email compatibility: PDFs are the most widely accepted attachment format for business communication

How to Preserve Formatting During Conversion

Table Borders and Cell Styling

Our converter preserves borders, merged cells, and basic cell formatting. For the best results, use explicit cell borders in Excel (not just gridlines) — gridlines are a display feature that doesn't always translate well to PDF.

Column Widths and Row Heights

If your spreadsheet has very wide columns that extend beyond the page width, they may be clipped in the PDF. Before converting, consider either narrowing columns, reducing font size, or switching to landscape orientation in your Page Layout settings.

Multiple Sheets

If your workbook contains multiple sheets, each sheet is converted to a separate section in the PDF. If you only need specific sheets, consider creating a copy with just those sheets before converting.

Charts and Images

Note that embedded charts and images in Excel files may not transfer perfectly in browser-based conversion. For chart-heavy spreadsheets, consider using the "Print to PDF" feature in Excel or Google Sheets as an alternative, then use OnlinePDFPro to compress the result.

Common Issues and How to Fix Them

🔴 Columns Cut Off on the Right Side

Fix: Reduce column widths, decrease font size, or set orientation to Landscape in Excel's Page Layout tab before exporting. Wide spreadsheets often need landscape formatting to fit within standard page widths.

🔴 Blank Pages in the PDF

Fix: Your spreadsheet may have stray content in distant cells. In Excel, press Ctrl+End to find the last used cell — if it's far from your actual data, delete those empty rows/columns. Then set a specific Print Area.

🔴 Numbers Displayed as #####

Fix: This means columns aren't wide enough in Excel. Widen the columns to show the full numbers before converting.

🔴 Large File Size After Conversion

Fix: Use our Compress PDF tool to reduce the output size. Table-based PDFs typically compress very well since they're mostly text data.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I convert .xlsx and .xls files?▼

Yes. Our tool supports both the modern .xlsx format (introduced in Excel 2007) and the legacy .xls format (Excel 97-2003). Both are converted with table formatting preserved.

Is my financial data safe during conversion?▼

Absolutely. OnlinePDFPro processes your spreadsheet entirely in your browser. No data is uploaded to any server. This makes it safe for payroll data, financial reports, and other sensitive business information.

Can I convert multiple Excel files at once?▼

You can convert one file at a time. Each file produces its own PDF. For batch processing, convert each file sequentially — the process is instant for each file.

Will formulas and calculated values appear in the PDF?▼

The PDF shows the calculated values (results), not the formulas themselves. If you need to preserve formula visibility, change cells to "Show Formulas" mode in Excel before converting.

Can I convert the PDF back to Excel later?▼

Yes! Use our PDF to Excel tool to extract tables from PDFs back into .xlsx format. The conversion preserves table structure and numerical data.
