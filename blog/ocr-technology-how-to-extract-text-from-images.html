What is OCR (Optical Character Recognition)?

OCR is a technology that converts images of text into actual machine-readable text. Instead of treating a document page as a single photograph, OCR analyzes the shapes of characters and translates them into letters, numbers, and symbols that computers can understand.

The concept dates back to the 1920s, but modern OCR — powered by machine learning and neural networks — has reached remarkable accuracy levels. Today's best OCR engines can recognize printed text with 99%+ accuracy and even handle some handwritten text.

Key Capabilities of Modern OCR

  • Multi-language support: Recognize text in 100+ languages including Latin, Cyrillic, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, and Indian scripts
  • Layout analysis: Understand multi-column layouts, tables, and mixed text/image content
  • Font recognition: Handle virtually any printed font, from serif to sans-serif, bold to italic
  • Noise tolerance: Work with imperfect scans that have slight skew, noise, or low contrast

How OCR Works: The Technical Process

Step 1: Image Pre-processing

Before analyzing text, the image is cleaned up: converted to grayscale, noise removed, contrast enhanced, and skew corrected. This step dramatically improves accuracy.

Step 2: Text Detection

The engine identifies regions of the image that contain text vs. images, borders, or whitespace. Modern algorithms use connected component analysis and edge detection.

Step 3: Character Segmentation

Text regions are broken down into individual characters. This is straightforward for well-spaced printed text but challenging for cursive handwriting or tightly-kerned fonts.

Step 4: Character Recognition

Each segmented character is compared against a trained model. Modern OCR engines like Tesseract (which powers OnlinePDFPro) use LSTM neural networks that consider not just individual character shapes but also the context of surrounding characters.

Step 5: Post-processing

Dictionary lookups and language models correct common recognition errors. For example, "th3" is corrected to "the" based on probability and context.

Factors That Affect OCR Accuracy

  • Image resolution: 300 DPI produces excellent results. Below 150 DPI, accuracy drops significantly
  • Text contrast: Black text on white background is ideal. Low contrast reduces accuracy
  • Font type: Standard printed fonts are recognized at 99%+. Decorative or handwritten fonts have lower accuracy
  • Document quality: Clean, flat scans outperform photos taken at angles with shadows
  • Language complexity: Latin-script languages have the highest accuracy. Complex scripts (Chinese, Arabic) may have slightly lower accuracy

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is OCR?▼

For clean, well-scanned printed text at 300 DPI, accuracy is typically 95-99%. Handwritten text and low-quality scans will have lower accuracy.

Can OCR read handwriting?▼

Modern OCR can handle neat, printed handwriting with moderate accuracy. Cursive handwriting remains challenging for most OCR engines.

What languages are supported?▼

Over 100 languages including English, Hindi, Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Arabic, Spanish, French, German, and many more.

Is OCR processing done on a server?▼

No. OnlinePDFPro uses Tesseract.js which runs entirely in your browser. Your documents never leave your device.

Can I OCR a multi-page PDF?▼

Yes. Each page is processed sequentially and all extracted text is combined in the output.
