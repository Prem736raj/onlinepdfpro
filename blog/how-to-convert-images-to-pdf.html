Why Convert Images to PDF?

  • Single file delivery: Instead of attaching 10 separate images to an email, send one organized PDF
  • Page ordering: Control exactly how images are arranged, something impossible with individual files
  • Universal format: PDFs open on every device without needing specific image viewer apps
  • Print-ready: PDFs produce reliable, properly-sized printouts on any printer
  • Portal uploads: Many government and university portals accept only PDF format for document submissions

Which Image Format Should You Use?

  • JPG/JPEG: Best for photographs. Compressed format with good quality-to-size ratio.
  • PNG: Best for screenshots, diagrams, and text. Supports transparency. Larger files than JPG.
  • WEBP: Modern format with excellent compression. Common in web downloads and screenshots.
  • BMP: Uncompressed bitmap. Maximum quality but very large files. Rarely needed.

Tips for Optimal Results

Compress Images First

For smaller PDFs, compress your images before converting. This is especially important for high-resolution camera photos.

Use Consistent Dimensions

For best-looking PDFs, use images with similar dimensions and orientation. Mixing portrait and landscape creates awkward page transitions.

Scan at Appropriate Resolution

For screen viewing: 150 DPI. For printing: 300 DPI. Higher adds file size without visible improvement.
